Science Facts

Explore the fundamental truths of our universe through data-driven insights and verified scientific phenomena.

🚀 Space Fridge

Extreme Physics

Neutron stars can spin 600 times per second.

Born from the explosive death of massive stars, these ultra-dense remnants rotate incredibly fast, emitting beams of electromagnetic radiation that we observe as pulsars.

Absolute Zero is Unreachable

By the laws of thermodynamics, it is impossible to cool a substance to exactly 0 Kelvin, though scientists have come within billionths of a degree.

CHEMISTRY

Water can exist in three states at once

At its "triple point" (0.01°C and a specific low pressure), water exists simultaneously as ice, liquid, and vapor.

More Science Facts

⚡

Lightning Is Hotter Than the Sun's Surface

A bolt of lightning can reach temperatures of about 30,000 K — roughly five times hotter than the surface of the sun at ~6,000 K.

🧬

DNA in Every Human Cell

If you stretched out the DNA from a single human cell, it would be about 2 meters long. The DNA in all your cells combined would stretch to Pluto and back several times.

🌊

Ocean Covers 71% of Earth

More than 70% of Earth's surface is covered by oceans, yet over 80% of these oceans remain unexplored and unmapped.

🔭

Light Takes 8 Minutes from the Sun

Light travels at approximately 299,792 km/s. Despite this incredible speed, sunlight still takes about 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.

🧲

Earth Has a Magnetic Field

Earth's magnetic field is generated by the movement of molten iron in its outer core. This field deflects harmful solar wind particles and enables compass navigation.

🫁

You Exhale Carbon, Not Just Air

With every breath you exhale, you release about 200 ml of carbon dioxide. Over a day, an average adult exhales roughly 200 grams of carbon.

Space Facts

Journey through the cosmos with mind-bending realities about our universe. Minimalist, immersive, and educational discoveries await.

Retrograde Rotation

Venus is the only planet in our solar system that spins clockwise. This retrograde rotation means the sun rises in the west and sets in the east.

✦

Neutron Stars

They can spin at a rate of 600 rotations per second after they are born in a supernova explosion.

Cosmic Ocean

There is a massive cloud of water vapor floating in space that holds 140 trillion times more water than Earth's oceans.

Lunar Drift

The Moon is Leaving

Every year, the Moon moves about 3.8 centimeters further away from the Earth. In a few billion years, Earth will not have a total solar eclipse.

☀️

The Sun's Core Temperature

The core of the Sun reaches approximately 15 million degrees Celsius, where hydrogen atoms fuse into helium through nuclear fusion.

🌌

The Milky Way's Size

Our galaxy is about 100,000 light-years across. Traveling at the speed of light, it would take 100,000 years to cross it from end to end.

🪐

Saturn Could Float

Saturn's average density is about 0.69 g/cm³, which is less than water (1 g/cm³). If you could find an ocean big enough, Saturn would float.

Nature

Understanding the natural world through verified scientific observation. Explore the intricate ecosystems, extraordinary wildlife, and dynamic forces that shape our planet.

Verified Nature Facts

WILDLIFE

Mycorrhizal Networks Connect Entire Forests

Trees communicate and share nutrients through an underground fungal network often referred to as the "Wood Wide Web," allowing mature trees to support younger saplings.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research
EARTH

The Amazon Creates Its Own Rain

Through a process called transpiration, the billions of trees in the Amazon rainforest release water vapor into the atmosphere, which condenses to form rain clouds over the basin.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research
CLIMATE

Ocean Phytoplankton Produce 50% of Our Oxygen

Microscopic marine organisms called phytoplankton perform photosynthesis, producing half of the oxygen in Earth's atmosphere, rivaling all terrestrial plants combined.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research
BIOLOGY

Tardigrades Can Survive in Space

Also called "water bears," tardigrades can survive exposure to the vacuum and radiation of outer space by entering a dormant state called cryptobiosis, making them Earth's hardiest animals.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research
OCEANS

The Deep Ocean is Largely Unexplored

More than 80% of the world's oceans remain unmapped and unexplored. The deepest point, the Mariana Trench, reaches about 11 km below sea level.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research
GEOLOGY

Lightning Strikes Earth 100 Times Per Second

Approximately 100 lightning bolts strike the Earth's surface every second, totaling around 8 million lightning strikes per day, playing a key role in the global electric circuit.

Source: Peer-Reviewed Research

Verified Technology Facts

AI & MACHINE LEARNING

The first AI program was written in 1951

Christopher Strachey wrote the first successful AI program, a checkers (draughts) playing algorithm, which ran on the Ferranti Mark 1 computer at the University of Manchester.

Source: Historical Computer Science Research
COMPUTING HISTORY

The first computer mouse was made of wood

Invented by Douglas Engelbart in 1964, the first computer mouse consisted of a wooden shell, circuit board and two metal wheels that came into contact with the surface it was being used on.

Source: SRI International Archives
NETWORKING

The first domain name registered was symbolics.com

On March 15, 1985, Symbolics Computer Corporation, a Massachusetts computer company, registered the domain name symbolics.com, marking the beginning of the commercial internet era.

Source: DNS Technical Documentation
SEMICONDUCTORS

Moore's Law has driven computing for 60 years

Gordon Moore observed in 1965 that the number of transistors on a microchip doubles approximately every two years, a trend that drove exponential gains in computing power for decades.

Source: Intel Corporation Archives
INTERNET

The World Wide Web was invented in 1989

Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web while working at CERN, publishing his proposal in March 1989 as a way to manage information through a system of hypertext documents.

Source: CERN Historical Records
ROBOTICS

The first industrial robot went to work in 1961

Unimate, the first industrial robot, was installed at a General Motors plant in New Jersey in 1961. It performed die-casting and welding tasks, paving the way for modern automated manufacturing.

Source: IEEE Robotics Historical Society
